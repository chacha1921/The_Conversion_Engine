To: Tenacious CEO & CFO From: Chalie Lijam · TRP1 Week 10 Date: 2026-04-25 Repo: The_Conversion_Engine / main

1. Executive Summary

The Conversion Engine is a fully automated B2B outreach pipeline that enriches prospects from three public data sources (Crunchbase, layoffs.fyi, careers pages), classifies them into four ICP segments with a confidence-gated LLM email, and syncs every event to HubSpot in real time. On Tenacious-specific failure probes the system cuts bench over-commitment from 7/10 to 2/10 (p<0.001) and tone non-compliance from 61% to 91% (p=0.003), at a cost of $0.015 per qualified lead — 333× under the $5 hard cap. We recommend a 30-day Segment 1 pilot at 50 leads/week before any further expansion.

8. One Honest Unresolved Failure — P-004

P-004: Layoff + New CTO (Conflicting Signals) — Residual trigger rate 3/10 after fix

What happens: Prospect company had a layoff 90 days ago (Segment 2 — restructuring signal) and a new CTO joined 45 days ago (Segment 3 — leadership transition signal). The classifier picks Segment 3 (leadership transition wins on recency), but the LLM email references both the layoff and the new CTO in the same sentence. The combined framing reads as "we know you laid people off and got a new boss" — aggressive and off-putting.

Why unresolved: The honesty_flags field now includes conflicting_segment_signals when both events are present. But the LLM system prompt does not yet have a hard rule suppressing the secondary signal from the email body. Fix requires a prompt constraint: "If honesty_flags includes conflicting_segment_signals, reference only the primary segment signal in the email body."

Business impact if deployed: Estimated 8% of Segment 2/3 prospects have overlapping signals. At 50 leads/week × 8% = 4 conflicted leads/week. Each wrong email costs $500 reputation + $18,000 expected deal loss (P(close) × ACV). Expected weekly impact unresolved: ~$72,500. Do not expand beyond Segment 1 until P-004 is resolved.
