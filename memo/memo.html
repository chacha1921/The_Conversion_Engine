The Conversion Engine — Decision Memo

To: Tenacious CEO & CFO From: Chalie Lijam · TRP1 Week 10 Date: 2026-04-25 Repo: The_Conversion_Engine / main

1. Executive Summary

The Conversion Engine replaces ad-hoc LinkedIn prospecting with a systematic pipeline that enriches every B2B prospect from three public data sources, classifies them into one of four ICP segments with a confidence-gated LLM email, and syncs every event to HubSpot in real time. On Tenacious-specific failure probes it significantly reduces the two highest-cost failure modes — bench over-commitment (−42pp, p<0.001 C-04) and tone non-compliance (−30pp, p=0.003 C-05) — while holding cost per qualified lead at $0.015, well below the $5 hard cap C-06. τ²-Bench retail held-out pass@1 is 0.70 vs baseline 0.7667 (−6.7pp, n.s.) — honest result reported; see §2. We recommend a 30-day pilot on Segment 1 (recently funded Series A/B) at 50 leads/week before expanding to further segments.

9. Public-Signal Lossiness

False negative — Quiet-sophisticated

A financial-services firm with a proprietary trading ML system that keeps its stack and team private (no AI job titles, no GitHub org, no public exec commentary). AI maturity score: 0–1. Agent either abstains or sends a generic opener. Tenacious misses a potential Segment 4 high-ACV deal to a competitor with a warm introduction. Estimated false-negative rate: ~15% of true Segment 4 companies are publicly silent.

False positive — Loud-but-shallow

A startup that posts "AI Engineer" and "ML Platform Lead" as aspirational roles with no actual ML infrastructure — the titles are recruiting theatre. Score: 2–3. Agent sends a Segment 4 capability pitch. The CTO knows they have no ML maturity and reads the email as tone-deaf overreach, deleting it or filing a complaint. Signal: job titles without ML stack evidence should reduce, not anchor, confidence.

10. Gap-Analysis Risks

Top-quartile benchmark irrelevant for product-embedded AI. The gap brief flags "no Named Head of AI" as a leadership gap. For a SaaS company whose product is AI (every engineer works on the model), this gap is a deliberate organisational choice, not a deficiency. The agent would pitch Tenacious as filling a gap that the prospect considers already solved internally. The signal in our data: DataSync AI's own gap brief flagged this pattern — CTO is ex-Google Brain but there is no separate "Head of AI" role. The pitch would be wrong for this company as a Segment 4 target.

Modern ML stack benchmark wrong for OT/IoT manufacturing. The top-quartile benchmark assumes cloud-native Snowflake + MLflow stacks. A discrete-manufacturing prospect whose bottleneck is OT sensor integration, not ML pipelines, would receive a pitch about data infrastructure that is architecturally irrelevant to their problem. The agent has no sub-niche discriminator for hardware-adjacent AI. Business impact: a well-researched but wrong pitch accelerates a "not relevant to us" response.

12. One Honest Failure — P-004 (Layoff + New CTO Conflict)

Probe: A company had a 15% workforce layoff 90 days ago and hired a new CTO 45 days ago. Before fix: 5/10 trials produced a conflicted email referencing both signals without resolving the tension. After fix: 3/10. C-20 Not resolved. The ICP classifier assigns the highest-confidence segment independently but the LLM system prompt does not explicitly resolve the conflict when both `layoff_event` and `leadership_change` are present. The agent sometimes produces copy that reads "we noticed your recent restructuring — and with your new CTO in place, this is a vendor-reassessment window", which a reader correctly identifies as the agent having two different scripts running simultaneously. Impact if deployed: $18K expected deal loss per triggered event. At 3/10 trigger rate on conflict-signal prospects, this is the highest residual risk in the probe library.

13. Kill-Switch Clause

The Tenacious CEO should pause this system if any one of these three conditions is met in any rolling 7-day window: C-21

  • Spam complaint rate >0.3% — measured via Resend `email.complained` webhook events. Rationale: Resend auto-suspends at 0.3–0.5%; preemptive pause preserves sender reputation. Threshold resets after manual review of flagged emails and prompt audit.
  • P-009 probe trigger rate >3/10 on the weekly automated probe run. Rationale: indicates bench_summary.json is stale or the injection prompt has regressed. Resume only after bench data is refreshed and probe returns ≤2/10.
  • More than 3 HubSpot contacts manually flagged "wrong signal" by SDRs in any 7-day window (via `tenacious_status=rejected` field). Rationale: field feedback is the ground truth that public-signal lossiness has crossed a tolerable threshold. Resume only after AI maturity scorer is recalibrated against the flagged examples.
